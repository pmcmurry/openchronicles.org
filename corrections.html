Corrections

Every Open Chronicles edition is versioned. Errors are corrected in new versions and logged here.

Report an error

If you find a mistake in any published edition — a mistranslation, a wrong name, date, or number, a typographical fault, a broken reference — please report it on the project's issue tracker (a free GitHub account is required), or email paul@openchronicles.org.

A useful report names:

  • the work and edition (source-faithful or reader’s);
  • the version (printed in the colophon and page footer);
  • the location — folio marker for source-faithful editions, chapter for reader’s editions, or a PDF page number;
  • what you believe is wrong, and — if you can — why.

Substantive corrections are verified against the source witnesses before they are applied. Corrected editions receive a new version number; the change is recorded below, and the edition's colophon is updated to show the current version.

Errata log

Volume 1 — Peter Tudebode

Source-faithful translation (The Journey to Jerusalem): version 1.0.0, unchanged.

Reader's edition (The Road to Jerusalem) — 1.0.0 → 1.1.0, 2026-07-17. An editorial-voice revision. The chronicle's own text — its events, numbers, speeches, dates, and religious language — is unchanged; the corrections are to editorial matter written in the editor's voice (the afterword, the adaptation note, and a few narrative asides).

  • Afterword, factual correction. A sentence in the afterword stated that Tudebode's God "burns a fortress of heretics with everyone inside it and receives the work as a mercy." The chronicle records the army's burning of a Pelagonia fortress (not a church) with its inhabitants, and offers no comment there; a doxology follows a different episode (the Vardar crossing). The God-as-agent and "mercy" framing have been removed. (The source-faithful translation never contained that phrasing and is unaffected.)
  • Editorial voice. The afterword's closing and several editor's-voice asides were rephrased to report the chronicle without passing modern moral judgment on either side, and the afterword now states the war's cause from both sides in the chronicle's own words. No event, number, speech, or the chronicle's own wording was changed.

Reader's edition — 1.1.0 → 1.1.1, 2026-07-17. A prose clarification of the afterword and one Chapter 14 sentence. The Pelagonia fortress burning remains accurately narrated in Chapter 3; the afterword no longer compresses it into a clause that could be misread as a church massacre. The afterword's closing is split into shorter paragraphs; Kerbogha's grievance is correctly attributed to his camp speech (not an oath); both cause-quotations match the source-faithful translation; and Chapter 14 restores the source's "fifty or sixty" and a cleaner attribution ("as the chronicle puts it").
